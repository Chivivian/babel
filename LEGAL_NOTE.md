### 2. Transformative Use Doctrine

Multi-language translation repositories qualify as **transformative works** when they:
- Add new utility (accessibility for non-English speakers)
- Create collaborative, annotated resources
- Serve educational purposes beyond the original work's scope

### 3. Archiving & Preservation

## Compliance Checklist

To ensure legal defensibility when open sourcing translations:

- [ ] **Attribution**: Credit original authors, institutions, and source documents
- [ ] **Non-Commercial**: Do not monetize translations or derivatives
- [ ] **Licensing**: Apply appropriate open license (e.g., CC BY-SA 4.0)
- [ ] **Transformation**: Add value through annotations, corrections, or collaborative features
- [ ] **Documentation**: Maintain records of source accessibility status
- [ ] **Good Faith**: Frame as educational/preservation initiative

## Recommended License

For translated works, we recommend:

```
Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International (CC BY-SA 4.0)
```

This license:
- Requires attribution to original authors
- Allows adaptation and sharing
- Ensures derivatives remain open
- Aligns with academic open access principles
